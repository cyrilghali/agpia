صلوات أخرى

مجموعة صلوات روحية من التقليد القبطي الأرثوذكسي.

صلاة التوبة

My Lord, God and Savior Jesus Christ, treasury of mercy and source of salvation, I come to You confessing my sins. I confess that with arrogance I have dared to defile Your holy altar with my sins. Now I have recourse to Your pity and tenderness, for Your mercy is infinite and You reject no sinner who comes to You.
Here I am, Lord, I confess that my sins are many and weigh heavily upon me. My strength has forsaken me. Turn not Your Face from me, Lord, lest I remain in dread. Keep me from Your wrath. Do not chastise me according to Your anger. Do not judge me according to my deeds.
Have mercy on me, Lord, for I am weak. Remember, Lord, that I am the work of Your hands, and be compassionate toward me. Do not put Your servant on trial, for no living being shall be justified before You. Clothe me with a new garment worthy of Your glory.
Forgive my sins and pardon me, that I may sing: Blessed are those whose iniquities have been forgiven, and whose sins have been covered. I confess my sins to You, Lord, and I do not hide my faults: You have taken away my sins. Amen.

صلاة قبل الاعتراف

Holy Father, who wishes the repentance of sinners, promising to accept them back; look, O Lord, at this moment, at a sinful soul who had erred and lost her way in the valleys of transgressions for many years, and wherein she was embittered and wretched for being removed from the fountain of her salvation.
Yet now she comes forth to Thee, asking to be cleansed from all defilement and filth that sullied her. Accept and reject her not, for whether Thou look at her with compassion and deal with her mercifully she will be cleansed and saved; or whether Thou neglect her she will perish.
Grant me grace to come near Thee with faith unshaken and full of hope to confess my trespasses and despise them. Let Thy spirit reprove me for my sins.
Enlighten my heart that I may see how greatly did I err, misbehave, and forsake the way.
Grant me determination to forsake evil that I may be established in Thy commandments and live to glorify Thy Holy Name. Amen.

صلاة بعد الاعتراف

I thank Thy goodness, O Lover-of-man, because Thou did not wish my perdition, but have awakened me from my neglect and guided me to Thy path.
Thou have brought me back from the valley of destruction to the safety of Thy bosom, so, fill me with hope and faith. I have come to thee a sick person to the Physician, hungry and needy to the Food of plenty, thirsty to the Spring of living Water, poor to the Source of riches, sinner to the Saviour, dead to the Fountain of Life; for Thou art my salvation, my healer, my life, my strength, my comfort, my happiness, and my rest is in Thee.
So help me, protect me, and teach me to put my will in Thy hands, that I may walk according to Thy will. Help my weakness that I May be firm and continue to be faithful to Thee till the end. Amen.

صلاة قبل التناول ١

O Lord, I am not worthy that Thou should come under the roof of my house, for I am sinful, but say Thou first a word and my soul shall be healed. Say unto my soul: "Your sins are forgiven." I am barren and wanting all righteousness, and have but Thy compassion, mercy and Love-of-Man. And Thou hast condescended from the heaven of Thine ineffable glory to our afflictions and accepted to be born in a manger.
Reject not, O my blessed Saviour to come into my lowly and afflicted soul that awaits Thy radiant presence. But accept to come into my soul to cleanse it.
O Thou who disdained not to enter into the leper's house and to heal him, forbid me not to approach Thy pure Body and Thy Holy Blood, O Thou who prevented not the woman, who was a sinner, from kissing they feet.
Let my communion be for partaking with Thee and for effacing all defilement, the mortification of my lusts, the doing of Thy Life-giving commandments, the healing of my soul and body from all sins, the acceptance of Thy gifts, the endwelling of Thy grace, the descent of Thy spirit for union with and abiding in Thee, that I may live for the glory of Thy Holy Name. Amen.

صلاة قبل التناول ٢

Jesus Christ, Lord of Life & God All Mighty, thou art the true live bread who descended from heaven and thou blessed with life those who receive your Holy flesh and blood.
Bestow upon thy servant heavenly blessings to enable me to receive your holy flesh and blood with due respect and humbleness.
Alert my heart with grace and grant me your salvation so that I may taste the sweetness of your heavenly grace which is wholly hidden in this sacred secret.
Grant me faith free from doubt or questioning so as to commence to you with unfailing faith and believing that this is your holy flesh and blood Emmanuel our Lord. Prepare me to receive Holy communion without falling into judgment but to unite me spiritually with you for forgiveness of my sins and for my accepted presence at your fearful kingdom. Glory to You, the Father and the Holy Spirit now and until eternity, Amen.

صلاة بعد التناول ١

My heart has been filled with joy and my tongue with rejoicing. Let my spirit glorify the Lord, and my soul rejoice in God my Savior. I have drawn nigh to Thee, O Lord, to clothe me with a radiant garment and make me worthy to enter into Thy wedding.
Let my union with Thee today be forever, for through it I increase and grow firm in virtue, wax strong in faith, and strengthen my hope. Let it become a sign of salvation, a raiment of grace, a garment of the new birth, a purity and righteousness of body and soul, a cleansing of love, an eternal happiness and joy, a good answer before Thy dreadful tribune.
I commit myself to Thy compassion to make me one with Thee and under Thy will. I call forth my mind and my senses to Thee. My will do Thou bless to obey Thy will.
Enliven my heart and awaken my conscience. Disperse the fantasies of the adversaries. Still the tempest. Walk with me and calm my terror. Quench my thirst, kindle the flame of Thy love in my heart. Abide with me for the day is far spent, and accompany me until daybreak. For Thou are my goal and my happiness. Thou alone, O Lord, forever. Amen.

صلاة بعد التناول ٢

O God, how great is the gift that you saved for your fearers. How sweet is your grace to those who love you.
I thank you my Lord for looking after me, You granted me this holy food that doesn't perish, by which you opened to us the way to eternal life.
Kindle the flames of your love in me, save the blessing of Your grace in my heart not for judgment or to fall into judging but to receive glory, purity of soul and body and to live with you, survive for you and continue with your grace. So guide me to your holiness and fill me with your grace and consecrate me with your soul. Glory to You, the Father and the Holy Spirit now and until eternity, Amen.

صلاة قبل الطعام ١

Blessed art Thou, O Lord who supported us from our youth and granted unto us Thy blessings, and prepared food for every creature; for the eyes of all await Thee, Thou who gave them their food in due season.
Thou open Thine hands and fill all living things. To Thee is due glory, praise, blessing and thanksgiving for the food that Thou hast prepared for us. Stretch forth Thy right hand and bless this food set before us for the nourishment of our bodies. Let it be for power and health of our lives.
Grant salvation, grace, blessing and purity to all those who partake thereof. Lift our minds to Thee at all times to seek our spiritual and eternal food. Grant that we may labour for the everlasting food which is for life eternal. Grant us to be partakers of Thy evening banquet.
Grant us the food of blessing, the cup of salvation, and fill our hearts with joy. Grant us a peaceful life, joy of the soul and health of the body.
Teach us to seek Thy pleasure in all things so that when eating, drinking or labouring, we do it all for the glory of Thy Holy Name. For Thine is the glory for ever and ever. Amen.

صلاة قبل الطعام ٢

Blessed are You O Lord, Who nourishes us from our birth, and grants us Your good things and prepares food for all people for the eyes of all hope in You for You give them their food in due season. You open Your hand and satisfy every living creature by Your goodness. Yours is the glory, the praise and the blessing. We give thanks to You for all that You have provided us as food which is set upon this table for feeding our bodies. Make it healing for our bodies. Our Father Who art in heaven…

صلاة بعد الطعام

We thank you O Lord, God the Pantocrator, for this dining table, which is a proof of your love for all of us your servants, who gained from the good of the earth.
We ask you our Master, bless what is left from us, as the blessing of the five loaves and the two fish which fed many gathering in the wilderness.
Be our help and strength, we who receive it & those who showed their mercy to us, those who served us, and those who ate with us.
Grant then O Lord your rewards of salvation. And compensate them for their hard work.
For Your Holy Name is blessed and full of grace, O Father, Son and the Holy Spirit, now and forever until eternity. Amen

صلاة قبل المذاكرة

Most blessed Lord, send the grace of Your Holy Spirit on me to strengthen me that I may learn well the subject I am about to study and by it become a better person for Your glory, the comfort of my family and the benefit of Your Church and our Nation. Amen
Christ, the true light, who enlightens and sanctifies every person coming into the world, let the light of Your countenance shine upon me (us) that I (we) may see Your unapproachable light; and guide my (our) steps in the way of Your commandments, through the intercessions of Your all-holy Mother and of all the Saints. Amen.

صلاة بعد المذاكرة

I thank You, Lord our God, that again on this occasion You have opened my eyes to the light of Your wisdom.
You have gladdened my heart with the knowledge of truth. I entreat You, Lord, help me always to do Your will.
Bless my soul and body, my words and deeds.
Enable me to grow in grace, virtue and good habits, that Your name may be glorified, Father, Son and Holy Spirit, now and forever. Amen.

صلاة التحليل الكهنوتي

We implore You and give You thanks, O Master and Lord Jesus Christ, Son of the living God, the Eternal. You dwell in the light before all ages: Light born of Light.
Enlighten our minds and our hearts. Enlighten our understanding that we may comprehend Your life-giving words.
And raise us from the darkness of the soul-destroying sin, and make us worthy to do good with uprightness and goodness, until Your second awesome Presence to judge the world, make us worthy to hear that voice full of joy, gladness, consolation, favor and happiness from Your divine mouth saying to us: "Come to Me, you blessed of My Father, inherit the Kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world."
Yes, Lord our God, make us fit on that day to be without fear, without defilement, without trouble, without falling and without blame. Do not seize us, Lord, according to the multitude of our iniquities; forgive us all our sins, our transgressions, our errors and our stumbling.
And remove from us and from the whole world death, famine, falling, corruption and exile, the sword of enemies, the conspiracy of Satan, the evil of fire and drowning, the intrigues of the impious, the injustice of authorities, the power of those who oppose us: may You reduce them to nothing.
O God, You are our Lord and our God. Be compassionate toward us. Do not cast us into the seas of sickness, nor into misfortune on our journeys, but make straight our paths, protect us, raise us from sighs, from sorrow, from negligence and from inattention.
Do not let the enemy and Satan become giants above us in hope, but elevate our intellect, and awaken our hearts from the sleep of distraction, and from the corruption of a vain existence.
O God, forgive our iniquities, forgive us our transgressions, do not remember our sins nor our errors. Do not be angry with us. Withhold Your wrath to the end. Have mercy on us, Lord, have mercy on us, for we are powerless and miserable, drowned in the seas of sins.
Behold, we cry to You, we prostrate before You, we confess to You and we desire You. Do not fail us in our hope, O my Master, for Your mercy; do not close the door of Your mercy toward us, but save us, guide us according to Your mercy, and let us hear Your joyful voice.
O God, purify us and purify our hearts from evil, malicious and defiled counsel. Lord, reduce to nothing in us dreams, phantoms and demonic thoughts. O God, raise us from every stumbling and every calamity. Establish in us, O God, patience, hope, love-agape, and the Orthodox Faith. Count us among the sheep placed at Your right hand.
Seat us among those who recline at Your heavenly banquet. Forgive us every iniquity, every sin and every error. Surround us with the armed ranks of Your radiant angels, for we have no salvation except in Your mercy, for we put our trust in You, our faith in You, and our hope in You.
Receive, Lord, the supplication that we address to You now and at all times. And all those who have asked us to remember them in prayers, liturgies, orations and commemorations (whether they be living or dead), remember us, Lord, with them, in the blessings of the heavenly Jerusalem, in the Kingdom of heaven.
Blot out, O God, our sins. To those who have sinned against us, forgive them as well as us, according to Your commandment. Lord, save all our souls. Lord, save Your people, bless Your inheritance, lead them and be with them to the end.
Save those who are in distress, tribulation, or need. Loose the captives from the bonds of demons. Satisfy the hungry with Your good things. Comfort those who are discouraged in their hearts and souls. Raise up those who have fallen. Strengthen those who stand. Bring back those who have gone astray. Provide for the needs of widows and orphans. Deign to help those who are in need.
Give Your blessing for the fruits of the earth, for favorable winds, and for the growth of trees. Raise the waters of the rivers. Bless the dews and the rains. Give sufficiency to those who are in need. Allow debtors to pay their debts.
Gather the dispersion of those who are separated. Accept the repentance of those who repent, and the confession of those who confess. Give understanding to the comforted. Render judgment, Lord, to those who commit injustice.
Fill our hearts with joy, gladness, comfort and exultation, so that, supplied with what is necessary in all things, we may abound in every good work that is pleasing to You.
Bless, Lord, the crops with heavenly graces. In Your goodness, bless the crown of this year. Bless, Lord, the sower, the cultivator, the planter and the reaper, as You blessed the crops of the sixth year for the children of Israel.
Bless, Lord, this our assembly and all the assemblies of the Orthodox peoples. Preserve for us the life and ministry of our father the Patriarch, our high priest. Save him, Lord, and save his people from all evil, all temptation and all affliction. Keep also his companions in the apostolic ministry: our fathers the metropolitans, our fathers the bishops, our fathers the abbots and our fathers the priests, our brothers the deacons, our fathers the monks and our brothers the lay people, as well as all those who have come here to participate in this prayer and in all Orthodox prayers imploring the forgiveness of their sins and mercy for their souls.
Bless us and bless them, absolve us and absolve them, forgive our sins as well as theirs, those past or to come, committed by ourselves or not, during the night or during the day, manifest or hidden.
Grant, Lord, that the places of assembly, the holy churches, the Orthodox monasteries, the elders who dwell therein, their deacons, their directors and those who dwell in them may endure. May hope, peace, love-agape and trusting obedience reside with them. Humble the evil enemy at our feet and at theirs. Leave him not a single share in us or in them, to strike from the right or from the left. Protect them by Your exalted hand and Your powerful arm. Save us, my Master, from evil temptations and from snares set, visible or invisible.
Remember, Lord, our fathers and our mothers, our brothers and our sisters, our families and our relatives, those who teach us, our spiritual and bodily children and all the children of baptism. Remember, Lord, those who have labored with us in communion. Remember, Lord, those who have given us to eat and to drink from the fruit of their toils and their labors, those who have lodged us in their residences and helped us. Repay them, Lord, with things that last instead of things that pass, with heavenly things instead of earthly things. Fill their houses and their storerooms with every grace. Render unto them, Lord, render unto them thirty, sixty or a hundred for one, with the forgiveness of their sins, in the Kingdom of heaven.
Remember, Lord, those who have done us good, us as well as our brothers the poor, by every blessing of beneficence, for the sake of Your holy Name. Bless them. Remember, Lord, those who occupy themselves with the sacrifices, the offerings, the wine, the oil, the firstfruits, the incense, the veils, the gifts, the books of reading, and all the vessels of the altar. Repay them, Lord, for their gifts, by the forgiveness of their sins. Establish them in this age with a joyful life, and in the age to come with eternal life.
Remember, Lord, our fathers and brothers who have preceded us in sleep and have departed for Rest in the Orthodox Faith in Christ. Lord, grant rest to their souls in the bosom of our holy fathers Abraham, Isaac and Jacob, in the land of the living, the Paradise of joy.
As for us, who are still alive, help us to obtain the salvation of our souls and direct our lives according to Your good will. Deign, Lord, to remember my weakness, my humility and my insignificance, I a sinful laborer, who am unworthy to stand before Your hands. Do not reject me because of my many sins and my defilements.
Raise up, O Lord, the horn of salvation of Your people by the sign of Your life-giving cross. Give, Lord, Your deliverance in distress, Your help in affliction and humiliation. Save us, Lord, and save all those who are in distress, and heal the sick of Your people.
And cause Your Christian people, Lord, those who are present with us and those who are absent, to be blessed and absolved by Your divine mouth, from the east to the west, and from the left to the right. Accept us as You accepted the thief on Your right hand on the wood of the Cross, and granted him the Paradise of joy.
Remember, Lord, the orphans and the widows, the excommunicated, the sick and those who have no one to remember them. Remember them and us in the heavenly Jerusalem.
Remember, Lord, those who rise, and those who are disabled, those who are in suffering, those who have had accidents while traveling on land, at sea, in steep places, valleys, roads, on the peaks of mountains, on hills or in caves. Remember also those who are in dungeons or prisons, the exiled, the expatriated, those who are bound or chained because of the chains of authorities and of demons. Bring them and lead them all to port safe and sound, victorious, in one piece, and with profit, for their soul, their body and their spirit.
My Lord Jesus Christ, grant that this prayer be accepted before You and that it be without hypocrisy, without pride, without arrogance, without presumption and without shame. Help us, O God, to do what pleases You. Help us to act according to Your commandments. Help us with the drunkenness of death, with what precedes it and what follows it, for You are a God who is good, patient, full of mercy and compassion.
O God, keep the door of Your Church open to us as in the ages and times past, and until the end of all times.
Have mercy on us, O God, according to Your great mercy, through the intercessions of the Pearl of purity and blessings, full of honor, our Lady of all, the pride of our race, the Virgin of pure virginity, Mary, and of the honored martyr Mark the Evangelist, the apostle who preached in the land of Egypt, of all the angels, of our fathers the prophets, the apostles, the martyrs, the saints, the ascetics, the champions and the athletes, and of all those who pleased the Lord in their good works from Adam until the end of the ages.
We prostrate before You, O Holy Trinity: the Father, the Son and the Holy Spirit, now and at all times, and for the ages of ages. Amen.
